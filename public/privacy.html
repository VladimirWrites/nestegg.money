Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 June 2026

nestegg ("the app") is a personal net-worth tracker built to keep your financial data private. This policy explains exactly what is and isn't collected. The short version: we cannot read your data, and we don't want to.

No account, no identity

There is no email, name, phone number, or password. Your only credential is a randomly generated account number, created in your browser. We never ask for, and never receive, anything that identifies you personally.

What the server stores

  • A one-way account hash derived from your account number (used only as a storage label — it cannot be reversed into your number).
  • An encrypted blob of your ledger that the server has no ability to decrypt.
  • A timestamp of your last sync.

How your data is encrypted

Your ledger is encrypted in your browser with AES-256-GCM. The key is derived from your account number using PBKDF2 and never leaves your device. The server only ever holds ciphertext, so it cannot see your assets, values, or totals.

What the server can still see

Zero-knowledge applies to the contents of your ledger, not to all metadata. Like any web service, the server (and our host, Cloudflare) can observe the size of your encrypted data, the time you sync, and your IP address in standard request logs. It cannot use any of this to read your figures.

A note on trust

Because the encryption runs in code your browser downloads from us, the privacy guarantee depends on that code not being tampered with. We serve the app as static files and keep it auditable. For maximum assurance you can review the source on GitHub and use Export JSON to keep an independent backup.

Third-party data

  • Exchange rates are fetched from the Frankfurter API (European Central Bank reference rates). No personal data is sent.
  • Security prices are looked up via a server-side proxy to Yahoo Finance. Only the public ticker symbol is sent upstream — never your identity, holdings, or account.
  • Hosting is provided by Cloudflare (Workers + D1).

No tracking

No cookies, no analytics, no advertising, no third-party trackers.

Data stored on your device

For convenience, your account number and a decrypted copy of your ledger are kept in your browser's local storage so you stay signed in. Logging out, or clearing your browser data, removes them from this device.

No recovery

If you lose your account number, your synced data cannot be recovered by anyone, including us — that is a direct consequence of the encryption design. Keep your number safe and export backups.

Deleting your data

You can clear everything on your device with "Reset" or "Log out". Your encrypted blob can be removed from the server on request from your client.
